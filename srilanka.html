Famous Places To Visit!!

Nine Arch Bridge

The Nine Arch Bridge is a stunning specimen of colonial-era railway buliding in Sri Lanka's hill region. The bridge, which is situated in the tiny village of Ella, is completely constructed of brick, stone, and cement, with no steal used. It was constructed in the early 20th century during the British colonial era and is renowned for its unique architecture and lovely scenery. The bridge crosses a beautiful valley encircled by lush green tea plantations, making it a favourite photography and wildlife location. Visitors can also watch as trains pass over the bridge, creating a spectacular sight as they traverse the steep incline and sharp turns. The Nine Arch Bridge is a must-see for anyone visiting Sri Lanka's mountainous region.

Temple of Tooth Relic

The Temple of Tooth Relic is a sacred Buddhist shrine located in the city of Kandy. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the world's most venerated Buddhist sanctuaries. The sanctuary is believed to house a Buddha tooth relic, which is kept inside a olden casket and paraded around the city during the annual Esala Perahera festival. The temple's architecture is a stunning blend of traditional Sri Lankan and South Indian styles, with intricate carvings and beautiful murala depicting scences from Buddhist mythology. Visitors to the temple can experience the spiritual and cultural significance of the site by participating in daily ceremonies and rituals.

Point Pedro

Point Pedro ia a coastal town located at the northernmost tip of Sri Lanka. It is known for its beaytiful beaches and as a popular fishing destination. The town has a rich cultural history and is home to several important Hindu temples, icluding the famous Kandaswamy Temple.Visitors to Point Pedro can enjoy te stunning natural beauty of the surrounding coastline and also explorre the town's historic landmarks, such as the Point Pedro Lighthouse, which offers fresh seafood delicacies that are popular among visitors. Overall, Point Pedro is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the natural beauty and rich culture of Sri Lanka's northern coast.

Galle Fort

Galle Fort is an ancient fortified sttlement on Sri Lanka's southern store. Te fort was built in the 16th century by the Portuguese and subsequently expanded by the Dutch, who used it as a center for the trade and commerce. Galle Fort is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of Asia's best-preserved instances of a fortified settlement. The fort's architecture is a beautiful blend of European and South Asian styles, with narrow streets, quaint vafes, and boutique shops selling local handcrafts. Visitors to Galle Fort can explore the historic landmarks, such as the Dutch Reformed Church and the Galle Lighthouse, and stroll along the fort walls to enjoy the stunning views of the Indian Ocean.
